Communication

In society, one of the biggest impacts of computer science is how communication is now handled. As information is now extemely easy to tranfer all around the world.

Before digital methods of communication, getting information between towns/cities would take days or weeks at a time. For example a letter to be sent from London to Edinburgh "might not receive a response for nearly two months" [1].

In contrast right now I can send a message to the another side of the world almost instantly. This allows todays world to be vastly interconnected allowing people to maintain friendships or relationships in vast or small distances.

How the methods of digital/analog communication has improved.

There has been many different methods before the concept of the internet was invented. To show how it got to this point we must understand the predecessors of communication using computing.

The telegram / morse code.

In 1838 the electrical telegram was invented this allowed information to be sent using morse code. This allowed communication between towns/cities easier.

The telephone

The first telephone invented was made in 1849. This allowed people to speak over long distances easier and can allow distant friends and or relatives to speak to one another.

The television

Finally the TV invented in 1927. This allowed the transfer of an audio and visual information over vast distances.

Conclusion

In summary the methods of communication has vastly improved in the past 200 years allowing for advances to instant communication across the world. This leaves a significant mark on society with computing today as many things we take granted are thanks to how communication has improved these past years.
